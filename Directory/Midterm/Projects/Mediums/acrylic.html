    BREATH

    24”/36” Acrylic on canvas


    Deep breath work relieves built up tension in the body and mind. To visually encapsulate this experience, this piece reflects on the ability to rely on intuitive instincts of survival to inspire others to value mindfulness in moments of stress. I chose to contrast feelings of darkness and despair with the experience of relaxation. Such darkness is represented by the deeper colour in surrounding proximity to the submerged head. As this piece was the second planned acrylic medium artwork I created, I found value in learning technical skills such as blending on canvas, colour mixing - understanding colour theory, and how to create visual movement through brushstrokes. Depicting movement through water, I found inspiration in both the natural flow and stillness of the medium. In crafting a sense of tranquility, I focused on portraying serenity through the subject's face, while the parallel horizontal plane of the water surrounding them extended this feeling throughout the entirety of the painting. My attention to these details aims to invite viewers into a contemplative space, encouraging reflection on the significance of intuitive self-care amidst life's complexities.

    Inner Voice

    24”/36” Acrylic on canvas


    This painting serves as a reminder to stop, reflect and feel. Leaning on others in times of hardship is important, yet the person who should constantly be there for support is yourself. Using a monochromatic palette serves as a symbol of unity, as there is wholeness felt from within when deciding to show kindness toward oneself. Depicting this figure anonymously further directs focus to the feeling of peaceful solitude being expressed.

    SPEAK!

    18”/12”/1” Acrylic on balsa wood


    The focus of this piece is verbal expression and the idea is that expression is not always soft or sad, it can be radically aggressive, not because of who it is directed at but the content of the message. I aim to depict a sense of clarity through a contrast in texture, colour and depth. The layered wood elements symbolize the evolving understanding of my identity as I release repressed emotions. During my creation process, I needed to deeply consider how an intense release of expression would project through a singular facial feature. Isolating the mouth as a symbol of emotional release allowed me to further examine how the form, colour and lines that make up a mouth could be exaggerated to explore my intent. Experimentation with this topographic style of sculpting led to a new appreciation for the use of expressive lines, working in three dimensions and the use of colour. Each of these aspects I included in my artwork entirely transfers my emotions.
